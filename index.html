A nightly prayer

The Examen

An Ignatian practice of looking back on the day with God, five quiet movements, each one a few minutes long. Move through them in order, at your own pace, before sleep.

I.Gratitude
II.Light
III.Review
IV.Sorrow
V.Grace

Movement I · Gratitude

Lord, for what am I most grateful today?

Let the day pass before you slowly. Notice the small gifts as much as the large ones. Rest a moment in thanks before moving on.

breathe with the flame

Movement II · Light

Lord, give me the light to see myself as you see me.

Ask for an honest eye, free of harshness and free of excuse, before looking back at the hours behind you.

breathe with the flame

Movement III · Review

Walk back through the day, hour by hour.

Where did you feel close to God, and where did you feel far? Notice the movements of consolation and desolation without judging them yet.

breathe with the flame

Movement IV · Sorrow

Lord, I am sorry. Help me see where I fell short.

Name what needs naming, gently and honestly. This is not shame, it is the beginning of mercy. Bring it to him as it is.

breathe with the flame
Possible areas for examination
This is a private aid to prayer and self-examination, not a confession and not a substitute for it. Nothing here is saved or sent anywhere beyond this one request. Use it or set it aside entirely.

Movement V · Grace

Lord, give me the grace I need for tomorrow.

Look forward, not back. Which virtue will tomorrow ask of you? Tap any of the seven to read about it before you choose.

Closing

Amen.

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end.
